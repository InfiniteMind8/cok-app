# Loom Script — Resident Walkthrough (~5 minutes)

**Goal:** Show a prospective resident what their day-to-day experience looks like inside the City of Karis app.

**Recording setup:** Full-screen browser, 1920×1080, zoom browser to 110% for readability. Use a demo account with a ~500 K Credit balance and at least 3–4 recent transactions.

---

## [0:00 – 0:30] Intro

> "Hey, I'm going to show you what it looks like to be a resident inside the City of Karis community platform. This is the app you'll use to manage your K Credits, pay for services, stay connected with the community, and track your property. Let's start from the beginning."

Open the app at `https://app.cityofkaris.com`. Show the sign-in screen briefly.

> "You sign in with your email — you'll get an invitation link from our team when you're onboarded."

Sign in. The app redirects to the Wallet tab.

---

## [0:30 – 1:30] Wallet Tab

> "This is your home base — your K Credit wallet."

Point out:
- **Balance card** at the top — the large K Credit number
- **Stat cards** below it: total received, total spent, pending settlements
- **Recent transactions** list — show 2–3 entries, each with type, amount, and counterparty

> "K Credits are the internal currency of the City. When you deposit fiat, the admin mints K Credits directly to your wallet. Everything inside the city — payments to vendors, rent, community fees — flows through here."

Scroll to show older transactions if available.

---

## [1:30 – 2:30] Settlement Request

> "When you want to convert K Credits back to fiat — say, to pay a vendor outside the city, or to settle part of your balance — you submit a settlement request."

Click the **Request Settlement** button.

> "I'll type in an amount…"

Enter an amount (e.g., 200 KRC).

> "Notice the fee preview updates in real time. The fee schedule is set by the admin — right now it's 1%. The community fund, operations, and developer share are broken out transparently."

Show the fee breakdown. Point to the net amount line.

> "Once I submit, it goes to admin for approval. I'll get notified when it's processed."

Submit the request (or cancel if using a live account). Show the confirmation state.

---

## [2:30 – 3:30] Property Tab

> "If you own or rent a property in the City of Karis, this tab is your property record."

Navigate to the Property tab. Show:
- **Property header** — code, type (Ownership/Rental), address
- **Photo carousel** — scroll through photos if present
- **Milestone strip** — tap/hover each node to see progress notes

> "The milestone strip tracks your payment progress. Each node is an installment — green means paid, amber means upcoming."

Click an installment node to show the detail popover.

---

## [3:30 – 4:15] Community Tab

> "The Community tab keeps you connected to what's happening in the City."

Navigate to the Community tab. Show the three sub-tabs:

**Updates tab:**
> "Admin publishes updates here — announcements, construction progress, events."

**Votes tab:**
> "When there's a decision the community votes on, it shows up here. You can cast your vote directly in the app."

Click a vote option to show the interaction.

**Issues tab:**
> "If something needs attention — a maintenance request, a question — you can raise it here and track the response."

---

## [4:15 – 5:00] Profile Tab

> "Finally, your profile — your digital identity inside the City of Karis."

Navigate to the Profile tab. Show:
- **Member ID** — the COK-XXXX number
- **KYC status** badge
- **QR card** — tap/expand the QR code

> "Your QR code is your identity card in the city. Vendors can scan it to pull up your wallet instantly — no need to share account numbers."

Expand the QR to full screen.

> "That's the resident experience in under five minutes. If you'd like access, reach out to our team and we'll send you an invitation."

---

## Recording Notes

- Turn off Slack/notifications before recording
- Use a demo account, not a real resident's account
- If demoing settlement, use a small amount and cancel rather than submit on the live DB
- Trim dead air at the start and end before uploading
